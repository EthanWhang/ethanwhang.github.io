Mary Zimmerman's "The Arabian Nights"

Furthering on the violent nature of Shahryar, here we will explore the role of Shahryar in Mary Zimmerman's adaptation of The Arabian Nights, a modern take on the relationship between Scheherazade and Shahryar.

Below are excerpts from Zimmerman's Arabian Nights performed at Berkeley Repertory Theater

Compared to other modern adaptations of the Nights, like the Ballet Russe, Zimmerman focuses more on the violence and potential for violence. In the Ballet Russe adaptation, the golden slave is graceful, and his love with the sultaness seems very real. Comparatively in Zimmerman's play Sharyar is exagerated in his threats and unstableness. This is underlined by the last scene, where the violence in Baghdad is put on display. Shahryar personifies the precarious place that Scheherizad, or the audience in Baghdad is in. The threat of death, and of violence is something that one must learn to live with and to look past, Zimmerman shows.
